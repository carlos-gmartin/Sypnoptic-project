Purse Seine

A boat locates a school of fish, then, using a small boat, the school of fish is surrounded by a net. Then they are pulled aboard with the fish inside. Purse seining only targets a particular school of fish after it has been located, so bycatch is extremely low. Because of this fisherman will deploy Fish aggregating devices(FADs). They can make seining much easier by reducing fuel use and time spent looking for schools of fish. FADs also attract other marine life and increase bycatch

Bottom trawl

Bottom trawls involve dragging a weighted net across the bottom to scoop up fish. The effect of bottom thrawl varies depending on the type of sea bed. Sandy and rock sea beds are not strongly effected as they can renegrate very quickely but sensitive habitats like deepwater coral or sponge gardens are negativly impacted by a bottom thrawl.

Midwater trawl

Midwater trawls pull a net through the mid- and surface water. To make mid water fishing easier acoustic techonology is used to locat the positon of the fish in accordance to the path of the boat.

Gillnet

Gillnets are a wall of nets that hangs vertically in the sea. Fish unknowingly swim into it and get stuck. Gillnets are commonly used in poorer countries as they dont require a boat with big engine to fish. Also gillnets have the most bycatch of any fishing methods.

Longlines

Longlines constsist of fishing line that are miles long behind a boat. It can be used when fishing at the bottom or mid water of the sea. If this type of fishing is not managed properly it can effect non traget fish and birds so fisherman using this techniques often use weighted long lines to allow it sink quicker.

Pole and line

A school of fish is first located and then fish bait is thrown. This trickes fishes into biting anything they see and at this ponit pole and line fishing is used to catch fish indiviadually. There is no concern over bycatch as only the target fish is effected.

Dredge

In dredge a meral rake is dragged across the ocean floar to harvest bivalves such as scallops, oysters and clams. The enviromental inmpact of dredgeing can vary greatly depending on the type of ocean floar- sensetive regions of the ocean floar like deepwater coral or sponge gardens are strongly negativly effected.
